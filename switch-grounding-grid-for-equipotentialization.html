Switch Grounding Grid for Equipotentialization

Victor Batista January 4, 2025

Small auxiliary meshes are usually used under the disconnector control boxes. The main reason for this network, according to the companies that adopt it, is to reduce the risk of electric shock and protect the operator.

In practice, a well-designed mesh for the entire substation protects people everywhere, even in rainy conditions and without adequate equipment, such as boots or gloves, avoiding direct contact with the gravel and the control box.

We carried out a brief study to evaluate how this auxiliary network affects the disconnectors in the switchyard and its effects on the risk of electric shock. In the entire substation where the study was carried out, the touch potential was considerably lower, however, the touch potential drops to less than 20 volts in the region where the disconnector auxiliary mesh was adopted.
