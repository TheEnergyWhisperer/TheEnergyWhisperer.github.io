Understanding Touch Potential in Substation External Fences: Importance for Safety and Compliance

Victor Batista January 4, 2025

Safety in electrical installations is a perennial concern, especially when it comes to substations and their external fencing. A crucial aspect of this safety is the management of touch potential on fences, which can impact the safety of personnel and the integrity of operations. The Impact of Ground Potential on External Fencing

When a short circuit occurs in a substation, the ground potential rise can extend beyond the area immediately enclosed by the main grid. This means that the area surrounding the substation can experience a significant ground potential rise, which can extend hundreds of meters beyond the grounding conductors. This phenomenon is crucial to understanding how safety should be managed around substation external fencing.

Touch Potential: What You Need to Know

External fencing, often referred to as perimeter fencing or property fencing, can present a significant touch potential. When touching the fence, the potential difference between the metal part of the fence and the ground can cause risks. According to NBR 15751, it is essential to evaluate the potential for touch considering the situation in which a person touches the fence while standing 1 meter away from a grounding point.

If the fence is grounded at multiple points, a varied potential for touch may arise along the fence due to a short circuit. To ensure safety, the highest possible potential for touch between the grounding points must be considered.

Importance of Compliance and Preventative Measures

Ensuring that external fences are properly grounded and that the touch potential is properly assessed is crucial to the safety of any electrical installation. Following standards and recommended practices, such as those established in NBR 15751, helps prevent accidents and protect both workers and the general public.

Safety in electrical installations is an aspect that should not be neglected. Understanding and correctly applying grounding standards and touch potential assessment are essential to maintaining a safe and regulatory-compliant environment.

Conclusion

Maintaining safety on the external fences of substations is vital to the protection of everyone involved. Adopting best practices and following established standards ensures not only compliance, but also operational safety. Always be aware of guidelines and potential assessments to protect your team and the community.
