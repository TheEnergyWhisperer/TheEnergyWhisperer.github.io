Best Practices for Cable Tray Design

Victor Batista January 4, 2025

Cable tray design is an essential practice in electrical infrastructure and network projects. It ensures the organization, safety, and efficiency of the system, as well as facilitating maintenance and future expansion. A well-executed design prevents problems such as overloading, interference, and cable damage, and promotes a safer and more efficient work environment. In this article, we will discuss best practices for cable tray design, covering everything from initial planning to execution and maintenance.

1. Proper Planning


Before beginning the installation, it is crucial to carry out detailed planning. This includes:

Needs Analysis: Assess the current and future demands of the system to properly size the tray. Consider the type and quantity of cables, as well as expansion needs.

Project Layout: Develop a layout that optimizes the use of space and facilitates access to the cables. The design should include the location of trays, supports, and cable entry and exit points.
Standards and Regulations: Ensure that the project complies with local and international standards and regulations, such as NBR 14565 (Cable Trays and Supports - Requirements and Procedures) and IEC 61537 (Supports for Cable Systems).

2. Choice of Materials


The selection of materials for the tray must consider several factors:

Type of Tray: There are different types of trays, such as perforated, solid and cable trays. The choice should be based on the type of cables, the environment and the need for ventilation.
Tray Material: Trays can be made of steel, aluminum or plastic. Steel is resistant and ideal for industrial environments, while aluminum is lighter and resistant to corrosion. Plastic trays are recommended for environments with low load and humidity.
Accessories: Use quality accessories, such as connectors, supports and fasteners, to ensure the stability and safety of the system.

3. Proper Installation

A well-done installation is essential for the systemâ€™s performance:

Spacing and Distance: Maintain adequate distance between trays and other components to ensure ventilation and avoid overloading.

Cable Management: Organize cables to minimize interference and facilitate access. Use dividers and supports to separate different types of cables, such as power and data.

Fixing and Support: Trays must be securely fixed, using appropriate supports and respecting the load specifications. Avoid overloading the trays, as this can cause damage to the cables and compromise safety.

4. Maintenance and Inspection


Regular maintenance is essential to ensure the longevity and efficiency of the tray system:

Periodic Inspections: Perform regular inspections to check the condition of the trays and cables. Look for signs of wear, corrosion or damage.

Cleaning: Keep the trays and cables clean and free of debris that can obstruct ventilation or cause other problems.

Documentation: Maintain detailed documentation of the system, including layout, material specifications, and maintenance records. This makes troubleshooting and future expansion easier.

Conclusion


Following best practices in cable tray design is essential to ensuring the efficiency, safety, and durability of electrical and network systems. Careful planning, proper selection of materials, proper installation, and regular maintenance are key elements to the success of any project. By adopting these practices, you not only protect your investment in the infrastructure, but also promote a safer and more productive work environment.
